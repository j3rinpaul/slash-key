About Slash Key

Slash Key is a 30-hour Hackathon organized by the IEEE Computer Society Kerala Chapter and hosted by the IEEE Computer Society Student Branch Chapter at Cochin University of Science and Technology, Ernakulam in May 2023. During hackathons, participants work together in teams to develop solutions to a specific problem or challenge.

About IEEE CS KS

The IEEE Computer Society Kerala Chapter is a professional organization for computer science and engineering professionals in the Kerala. It aims to promote the advancement of computer science and engineering in region by providing oppurtunities for education, networking and professional development. The society organizes a variety of events, including the technical talks, workshops, seminars, and conferences, to facilitate the exchange of knowledge and ideas among professionals in the field. The society also provides support for students pursuing computer science and engineering education through various initatives, such as student chapters, student competitions and scholarships. Thus, the IEEE Computer Society Kerala Chapter plays a viral role in advancing computer science and engineering in Kerala.

About IEEE CUSAT SB

The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) has a student branch at the University of Cochin Science and Technology (CUSAT) in Kerala, India. The student branch was established in 1986 and is the fourth student branch chapter in the IEEE Kerala section. The majority of its members are undergraduates from the School of Engineering. As IEEE student members, they receive technical and professional benefits at subsidized rates. The branch is supervised by a faculty member of the university who is an IEEE member. With over 200 members, the branch is known for providing excellent opportunities for career building and developing critical skills. Despite challenges, the branch has continued to grow over the years and has become one of the finest branches in the area.

Pre Events

Idea Pitching Competition

Participants will have the opportunity to present their ideas in a short pitch format, highlighting the problem they are solving, and the potential impact of their project.

Pre-Hackathon

A mini version of the main Hackathon event, typically lasting around 10 hours. It provides participants with an opportunity to experience the process of developing a solution to a specific problem within a shorter time frame. Participants work in teams to ideate, prototype, and present their solutions, with the event serving as a practice run for the main Hackathon. The pre-hackathon allows participants to get a feel for the format and build their skills, ensuring they are well-prepared for the main event.

Code of Conduct

IEEE SYP CS KS is dedicated to creating a safe and welcoming environment for all participants at our 30-hour hackathon competition. We expect all participants, including attendees, volunteers, mentors, judges, sponsors, and organizers, to comply with this Code of Conduct. We believe that by creating an inclusive and respectful environment, we can foster collaboration, learning, and innovation.

Applicability

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces related to the IEEE SYP CS KS hackathon competition, including online platforms, hackathon venues, workshops, social events, and communication channels.

No plagiarism or reusing past work

We encourage participants to submit projects that are developed during the duration of the hackathon competition. If a participant decides to submit a project that includes re-used code or has previously been submitted to another hackathon, they must disclose such use and its extent with the submission. If a submitted project is found to have re-used code that was not disclosed, the organizer may ask the participant to point out similarities and differences between the old and new work, and may disqualify the submission from winning any awards automatically.

No discrimination

IEEE SYP CS KS is committed to providing a safe and inclusive environment for all participants. Discrimination of any kind, based on gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, political views, previous hackathon attendance, computing experience, or choice of programming language or tech stack, will not be tolerated.

No harassment

IEEE SYP CS KS does not tolerate any form of harassment, including offensive discriminatory verbal comments, public display of sexual material, deliberate intimidation, stalking, wilful disruption, inappropriate physical contact, unwelcome sexual advances, and the taking of photographs and audio/video recordings without the subject's consent.

No recording without consent

While photography and videography is allowed, participants must be given a reasonable chance to opt-out from being photographed. If they object to the taking of their photograph, comply with their request. If they express their disapproval after the photo or video has been captured, please delete it from your records, and in case it has been shared online, take reasonable steps to retract it from social media as well.

Creation of a safe space

No sponsors, partners, or participants shall use sexualized images, activities, or other material at the hackathon competition for any non-permitted purpose. The use of sexualized clothing/uniforms/costumes and anything that creates a sexualized environment is prohibited.

Intellectual Property

Participants own any developments made by them during the hackathon competition, and all rights, titles, and interests in those developments, including the intellectual property rights, shall belong to the participant. By posting their submission on IEEE CSKS platforms, participants are granting IEEE CSKS a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, distribute, display, and reproduce the submission only to the extent required by IEEE CSKS to provide services on its platform. IEEE CSKS will never attempt to steal or use a participant's creations exploitatively.

Always report

If a participant notices any violation of this Code of Conduct or finds otherwise suspicious behavior or has any concerns, they should contact a member of the hackathon organizing committee immediately. We are committed to addressing all concerns and assisting participants in feeling safe and comfortable during the hackathon competition.

Consequences of violations

In case any participant violates this Code of Conduct, the organizer may, at their own discretion:

  • Expel them from the hackathon competition with no refund.
  • Block their access to IEEE CS KS resources.
  • Report their behavior to local law enforcement.
